# Finance Trend Rubric

## Status Definitions

- `strengthening`: broader reliable-source spread, stronger price confirmation, or higher official relevance than prior runs.
- `weakening`: weaker reaction, narrowing breadth, lower-quality support, or failure to extend.
- `reversal`: price direction, policy tone, or evidence changes against the prior interpretation.
- `confirmed`: a prior watch item receives official, company, exchange, or reliable independent confirmation.
- `falsified`: a prior thesis is contradicted by official evidence, market data, or later disclosure.
- `unchanged`: no meaningful new information or price/sentiment change.

## Comparison Windows

- Intra-run: compare early and late items inside the current 30-hour window.
- Prior-run: compare current bundle with the latest 1-3 runs.
- Weekly context: compare current bundle with the latest 5-7 runs when available.

## Signals To Track

- Source intensity: number of sources and source grades discussing a theme.
- Asset reaction: related index, stock, ETF, futures, FX, rates, or commodity movement.
- Cross-asset confirmation: e.g. policy easing story plus lower yields plus stronger growth assets.
- Futures confirmation: e.g. corn and soymeal rise together, or hog futures move against feed cost pressure.
- Stock confirmation: multiple stocks in the same chain move with confirmed drivers, or one leader's move spreads to suppliers/peers.
- Stock warning: hot names rise on rumors, receive inquiry letters, or move without peer confirmation.
- Technology rotation: AI infrastructure, semiconductors, software, internet platforms, consumer electronics, smart vehicles/robotics, and digital infrastructure move in different directions.
- Global-to-China technology transmission: US AI or semiconductor moves transmit to A/H technology names, or China-specific policy/earnings causes independent divergence.
- Follow-up resolution: yesterday's unresolved watch item confirmed, denied, or still pending.

## Path And Candidate Priorities

Prefer 3-6 high-signal observations. Each must identify the comparison window and distinguish sequence from cause.

Escalate a trend into a research candidate only when history changes the explanation, such as a failed high-turnover rebound preceding a later shock, or a recurring policy headline failing to produce the expected asset response. Include at least two alternative mechanisms and a feasible historical or cross-sectional benchmark.
